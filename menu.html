Our Menu


Starters

Sambusa

Beautiful triangular shaped Somali sambusa, filled with ground beef, onion mixture, potatoes and green onions

Buur

Buur is a sweet fried bread that is made with flour, oil, milk and eggs, cardamom powder and with the help of the baking powder.

Baajiye

Tasty Somali black-eye fritters, this snack is served green chili sauce.

Àmàlà

A delicious staple swallow food native to the Yoruba ethnic group of Southwestern Nigeria. It is made of yam, cassava flour, or unripe plantain flour.

Plantain Chips

Chips made of roasted/fried plantains. Is a great starter before any meal.

Kokoro

A delectable traditional nigerian crunchy cornmeal snack, made from a specially prepared dough consisting of corn meal,sugar and water, rolled to shape and deep-fried.

Somali Mains

Bariis iyo suugo

Somali rice that is cooked with onion, garlic, and xawaash and served with Somali sauce toppings and roasted lamb, camel, goat, chicken or beef and most importanly banana on the side.

Baasto iyo hilib

Traditional Somali pasta made with the sauce of meatballs, tomatoes, potato slices, garlic, onion, sunflower oil, and xawaaji (a Somali spice mixture). Served with roasted lamb, camel, goat, chicken or beef and most importanly banana on the side.

Sabayad iyo maraq

Sweet delicious somali chapati served with meat sauce of your liking could be lamb, camel, goat, beef, chicken or fish.

Nigerian Mains

Jollof Rice

A flavorful and aromatic one-pot dish made with long-grain parboiled rice, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. It's known for its vibrant red color and is a staple in many West African countries. Our jollof rice is cooked to perfection, with a rich tomato-based sauce and a medley of spices that create a burst of flavors in every bite.

Efo Riro

A popular Nigerian dish made with leafy green vegetables cooked in a rich and spicy tomato-based sauce. We prepare our Efo Riro with a variety of vegetables such as spinach, kale, or collard greens, combined with onions, tomatoes, bell peppers, and a blend of spices. It's a hearty and nutritious dish that is typically served with rice, pounded yam, or fufu.

Egusi Soup

A flavorful stew made with ground melon seeds (egusi) and leafy green vegetables. The melon seeds are roasted and ground to create a thick and nutty base for the stew, which is then cooked with a variety of meats or fish, onions, tomatoes, and other spices. Our Egusi Soup is a delicious and satisfying dish that is often served with pounded yam, eba, or rice.

Suya

A popular West African street food made with marinated and grilled skewered meat, typically beef, chicken, or lamb. The meat is coated with a spicy peanut-based sauce and grilled to smoky perfection. Our suya is packed with flavor, with a perfect balance of spices, and is served with a side of spicy peanut sauce for dipping.

Ewa Agoyin

A spicy stew made with mashed black-eyed peas, typically cooked with palm oil, onions, and a blend of spices. Our Ewa Agoyin is a delicious and hearty dish with a smooth and creamy texture, and it's served with a side of spicy stew that adds an extra kick of flavor.

Okra Soup

A popular West African soup made with okra, a slimy green vegetable that thickens the soup and gives it a unique texture. Our okra soup is cooked with a variety of meats or fish, onions, tomatoes, and spices, creating a rich and flavorful broth. It's typically served with rice, fufu, or eba.

Somali Desserts

Shushumow

Sweet tasty Shushumow is a fried pastry made with flour, eggs, and water or milk tossed in simple sugar syrup.

Malawah

A mouthwatering sweet Somali style crepe made with unleavened dough and can be either sweet or savory. Sweet malawah is often served with honey, jam, or syrup, while savory malawah is usually topped with eggs.

Doolshe Buuro

Doolshe Buuro is a sweet delicate Somali rice pudding made with short-grain rice, milk, and sugar.

Nigerian Desserts

Puff-puff

A popular Nigerian dessert, Puff-puff is made by spicing batter, especially with diced fresh pepper, before deep-frying in vegetable oil.

Shuku shuku

Shuku shuku are prepared by mixing coconut flakes with caster sugar, egg yolks, and flour. When the mixture achieves a doughy texture, it's ready for baking. The shuku shuku balls come out of the oven with an irresistible aroma that no sweet dessert lover can resist!

Akara

A very famous nigerian savory dessert. Akara is prepared from bean flour and can be spiced to taste with onions, pepper, crayfish, it is then molded into small balls and deep-fried. It can be enjoyed with pap/custard or served with potato/yam/plantain fries.
